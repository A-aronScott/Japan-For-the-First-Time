Tokyo

Tokyo, the capital of Japan and home to two major international airports, Narita and Haneda, it an amazing and convenient place to start your trip. It is also a city that provides a great mix of modern and traditional. Here are some of the best spots to visit in this amazing city.

  • Tokyo Skytree, the observation deck provides a great view of Tokyo and on a clear day you might see Mt Fuji in the distance.
  • Senso-ji Temple, the oldest temple in the city and one of its most popular attractions.
  • Shibuya, the heart of Tokyo's modern side and home to the famous Shibuya Crossing.
  • Ginza, for high end shopping and dining this is the place

Kyoto

The former capital of Japan, this city alone has enough to offer to fill and entire trip. Try to spend a few days here during your stay and see as many of these must see locations as you can.

  • Fushimi Inari, the head shrine for the god of rice and one of the most visited shrines in Kyoto.
  • The Golden Pavilion, a Zen Temple whose top two floors are covered in gold leaf
  • Kiyomizu-dera(Temple), The origins of this temple built in 780 are shrouded in legend
  • Gion, Kyoto's most famous geisha district offers a look into historical Japan

Other Places to Visit

While Tokyo and Kyoto are the most recommended desinations for first time visitors to Japan there are many other spots to visit if you have the time, or to keep in mind for your next visit. Here are a few.

  • Osaka, Japan's second largest city is known for its food and nightlife
  • Hiroshima, a city with a tragic past but a hopeful future, it is home to the Peace Memorial Park and Museum
  • Nara, home to the famous Nara Deer Park and Todai-ji Temple
  • Hakone, a popular hot spring resort town with views of Mt. Fuji
